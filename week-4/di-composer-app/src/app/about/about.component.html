Claude Debussy



Born Achille Claude Debussy in France on August 22, 1862, Claude Debussy was a French impressionist composer. At the age of ten he attended one of France’s prominent music colleges, the Conservatoire de Paris, where he studied piano and discovered a passion for innovative composition. Debussy received international fame in 1902 with the opera Pelléas et Mélisande. His most famous works include Claire de Lune, La mer, Suite begamasque and La fille aux cheveux de lin, among others. He died in 1918 at the age of fifty-six.
