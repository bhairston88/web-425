Neil Gaiman

Born Neil Richard Gaiman in Porchester, Hampshire, England, Gaiman is an award-winning English author, comic book creator and screenwriter. Best known for his work in science fiction and fantasy, Gaiman is also a comedic writer. He is the first author to win both the Newbery and Carnegie medals for the same work, The Graveyard Book (2008). His other notable works include American Gods, Stardust, The Sandman and Good Omens which he co-wrote with author Terry Pratchett.
